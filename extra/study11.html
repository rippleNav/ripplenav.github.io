The general idea here is for you to practice the seven steps from your previous modules, but now you get to use the real  RippleNav. 

The trick is to split your attention with 90% on a subject in your mind and only 10% on the  RippleNav  Most newcomers will have ALL their attention on the  RippleNav  because it is new and interesting, especially the first time you see it respond to a thought you had.

You need to 'observe' the  RippleNav  but 'work' on your inner world because that is where the things are that we want to fix. Spend a few hours doing this until you can do the seven steps without having to use the 'helper' we put below the  RippleNav. 

The  RippleNav  is 'smart' in that it watches itself and knows when it is being 'used' and keeps track of how long it is being used for. Just leaving it idle will not count as being used.

The sensitivity setting ( The green button in the grey bar ) of the  RippleNav  can be used to increase the 'detection' size of UTE's and other motions. Increasing it too much will make the device unusable because everything will be in motion. Experienced users will operate the device with sensitivity in the range of 10 - 25, but it's up to you. Do what works for you.

To find UTE's just think about times when you were upset, depressed, angry, did something you regretted, said something you regretted etc. Any thought that generates a response on the  RippleNav  is a UTE. You just need to 'find it', put your attention on it, find its Magic Number.

There are more effective ways of finding UTE's which we explain in the next module but we want you to first get some practice here.
