This web page is a simplified version of the main site, designed for researchers and individuals with a scientific interest who wish to investigate correlations between thought and pseudo-random number generation (PRNG), without delving into theoretical frameworks about consciousness.

The core observation is a potential correlation between mental focus and the motion of the visual indicator. Please read the complete instructions before attempting the experiment.

Before You Begin

This experiment is inherently subjective and requires genuine curiosity about consciousness, personal introspection, and patience. Optimal results occur when participants approach the process with sincerity and openness to possibilities that may challenge conventional assumptions. This is an exploration of personal discovery, not debate.

Understanding the Mechanism

The indicator bar responds to a PRNG whose output appears to exhibit correlation with focused thought, particularly when that thought carries emotional weight or significance. While this may seem impossible to casual observers, the phenomenon has been repeatedly observed under these specific conditions. The primary application is introspective—identifying subconscious patterns and hidden mental content.

Interpreting Movement

  • Rightward motion: Typically indicates engagement with or movement toward the thought or memory being contemplated
  • Leftward motion: Usually indicates avoidance or resistance to confronting the topic—a "moving away from" response
  • Return to neutral: After rightward motion completes, the indicator must return to center, producing leftward movement that carries no interpretive significance

What you observe is the deviation in PRNG output average, apparently correlated with focused thought. While this challenges conventional understanding, empirical observation remains the foundation of this inquiry.

Experimental Protocol

  1. Set sensitivity to 30 on the control interface
  2. Maintain observer perspective: Approach with genuine curiosity rather than predetermined skepticism. Your expectation influences observation—deciding it's impossible may prevent the correlation from manifesting
  3. Observe passively: Do not attempt to "move" the indicator with intention. Simply watch while maintaining mental focus
  4. Focus on emotionally significant memories: Recall past experiences with strong emotional content—either traumatic or highly meaningful moments
  5. Note patterns: Emotionally charged memories frequently correlate with rightward motion

Important Considerations

Apprehension about the experiment producing results often manifests as leftward motion—resistance to engaging with the phenomenon itself. This meta-level avoidance is itself a meaningful observation about consciousness and belief.
